What This Is

This teleprompter scrolls script lines at the exact timing needed to sync voice-over audio with the Pad Logic marketing video animations. Each line lights up when you should start reading it and dims when you should be done. Your job is to match the scroll.

How to Read the Screen

  • The orange bar on the left marks the "read zone" - this is where your eyes should focus.
  • The white line is the one you should be reading right now.
  • The gray line below it is your preview of what comes next.
  • Dark lines above are done. Dark lines below are coming up.
  • Small timestamps above each line show your cue-in and hard-out times.
  • The orange timer at top center is your running clock. If it turns red, you have gone past the speech end time.
  • The thin progress bar at the bottom shows overall clip progress.

Delivery Style

  • Calm, confident, oilfield-credible. You are talking to operators and production engineers, not selling a car.
  • Never rushed, but very disciplined on timing. Each line gets exactly 3.5 seconds of spoken time.
  • There is a 0.4 second gap between lines - end each line cleanly so that gap is preserved.
  • Where you see ellipses (...), give a brief natural pause - do not skip them, but do not over-dramatize either.
  • Clip 1: Tightest pacing. Keep pauses crisp. Let the ellipses breathe but no extra dead air.
  • Clip 2: Educational and smooth. Avoid sounding salesy early - let the confidence build as the system explanation unfolds.
  • Clip 3: Urgent but not frantic. Build contrast line by line: left side reactive, right side controlled.

Recording Workflow

  • Step 1 - Practice: Set the speed slider to 0.8x and run through the clip once or twice without recording. Get comfortable with the rhythm.
  • Step 2 - Record: Set the speed back to 1.0x. Click the Rec button (or just click Play and record externally). A 3-second countdown will appear, then the scroll starts.
  • Step 3 - Follow the white line. Finish each line before it dims. If you fall behind, the timer turns red.
  • Step 4 - File saves automatically when the clip ends or you click Stop. It downloads as a .webm file named for the clip.
  • Step 5 - Re-record full clips only. If you need a pickup, re-record the entire clip from the top so pacing stays consistent. Do not splice single lines.
Timing is critical. The 0.5 sec silence at the head and 2.0 sec tail at the end are built into the scroll timing. The video animation engine expects voice-over to land exactly on these cue marks. If you drift even one line, the voice will be out of sync with the animation for the rest of the clip.
